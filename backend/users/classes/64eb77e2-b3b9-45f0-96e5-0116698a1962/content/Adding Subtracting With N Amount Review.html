Module 6: Review of Adding and Subtracting with N Amounts

This review covers key concepts for addition and subtraction with multiple numbers (N amounts).

Adding with N Amounts: Key Points

Adding with N amounts means putting together more than two numbers to find the total.

Sometimes we add three, four, or more numbers together.

We can also have a missing number represented by a blank (like __) to solve for.

Example: 1 + 2 + 3 + __ = 10. The missing number is 4.

How to solve:

  • Add the known numbers first
  • Use counting objects (blocks, cubes, stickers) for each number
  • Use a number line to hop forward step by step
  • Check if the total matches the number on the right side of the equation

Tens and Ones Reminder:

  • Add the ones first, then the tens to get the total

Why it’s important:

  • Work with bigger numbers
  • Understand addition patterns
  • Solve problems with multiple steps
  • Prepare for subtraction, multiplication, and more advanced math

Subtracting with N Amounts: Key Points

Subtracting with N amounts means taking away several numbers in a sequence and finding the total or missing number.

Sometimes we subtract more than two numbers in a row.

We can also have a missing number represented by a blank (like __) to solve for.

Example: __ - 5 - 3 = 2. The missing number is 10.

How to solve:

  • Start with the known numbers and work backward to find the missing number
  • Subtract step by step from left to right
  • Use objects or drawings to represent each subtraction
  • Use a number line to move backward in steps

Tens and Ones Reminder:

  • Subtract the ones first, then the tens
  • Sometimes “borrow” is needed to make subtraction work correctly

Why it’s important:

  • Work with bigger numbers and sequences
  • Understand subtraction patterns
  • Solve multi-step problems
  • Prepare for more advanced math like multiplication and division

Tips for N-Amount Problems

  • Break the problem into smaller steps and solve one number at a time
  • Visualize numbers with objects or drawings
  • Check your answer by reversing the operation (addition ↔ subtraction)
  • Practice with blanks to improve problem-solving confidence
  • Always handle tens and ones carefully to avoid mistakes
