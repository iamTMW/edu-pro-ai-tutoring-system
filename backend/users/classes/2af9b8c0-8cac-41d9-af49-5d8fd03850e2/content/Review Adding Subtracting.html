Module 5: Review of Basic Addition and Subtraction

This review covers the key ideas and important tips for addition and subtraction.

Addition: Key Points

Addition means putting things together to find out how many we have in total.

If you have some things and then get more, addition helps us find out the new amount.

We show addition with the + sign and the answer with =.

Example: If you have 4 apples and get 3 more, you have 7 apples.

Ways to add:

  • Counting up (start at one number and hop forward)
  • Using fingers
  • Using a number line
  • Using objects (blocks, stickers, cubes)

Tens and Ones Reminder:

  • Some numbers have tens (20, 30, 40) and ones (1, 2, 3).
  • Add the ones first, then the tens.

Why addition is important:

  • Counting toys
  • Adding time
  • Counting money
  • Playing games
  • Sharing snacks

Subtraction: Key Points

Subtraction means taking away or finding how many are left.

If you have something and you use, lose, or give some away, subtraction helps us find how many remain.

We show subtraction with the – sign.

Example: If I have 10 cookies and I eat 3, I have 7 cookies left.

Ways to subtract:

  • Counting backwards
  • Using a number line
  • Taking away physical objects
  • Thinking in terms of “How many left?”

Tens and Ones Reminder:

  • Sometimes we need to “borrow” when subtracting.
  • Take away ones from ones, then tens from tens.

Why subtraction is important:

  • Counting how much is left
  • Comparing amounts
  • Figuring out differences
  • Solving everyday problems

Tips for Both Addition and Subtraction

  • Understand the problem before starting.
  • Use objects or drawings to visualize numbers.
  • Check answers using the inverse operation (addition ↔ subtraction).
  • Practice with blanks and multiple numbers to build confidence.
  • Break big numbers into tens and ones to simplify calculations.
